Dinosaurs

Meet some of the beasts that once roamed our planet!

Iguanodon "iguana-tooth" Iguanodon were bulky herbivores that could shift from bipedality to quadrupedality. These animals had large, tall but narrow skulls, with toothless beaks probably covered with keratin, and teeth like those of iguanas, as the name suggests, but much larger and more closely packed. 1822 Iguanodon
Diplodocus "double beam" Diplodocus were very large, long-necked, quadrupedal animals, with long, whip-like tails.Their forelimbs were slightly shorter than their hind limbs, resulting in a largely horizontal posture. The skeletal structure of these long-necked, long-tailed animals supported by four sturdy legs have been compared with suspension bridges. 1877 Diplodocus
Brontosaurus "thunder lizard" Brontosaurus was a large, long-necked, quadrupedal animal with a long, whip-like tail, and forelimbs that were slightly shorter than its hindlimbs.The largest species measured up to 21–22 m (69–72 ft) long from head to tail and weighed up to 15–17 t (17–19 short tons). 1879 Brontosaurus
Tyrannosaurus "the tyrant lizard king" Tyrannosaurus was a bipedal carnivore with a massive skull balanced by a long, heavy tail.Relative to its large and powerful hind limbs, the forelimbs of Tyrannosaurus were short but unusually powerful for their size, and they had two clawed digits. 1874 Tyrannosaurus
Raptorex "robber king" The only known Raptorex specimen shows the same basic proportions as juvenile tyrannosaurids: a comparatively large and solidly-constructed skull, long legs with adaptations for running, and tiny, two-fingered forelimbs. 2009 Raptorex
Stegosaurus "roof-lizard" The quadrupedal Stegosaurus is one of the most easily identifiable dinosaur genera, due to the distinctive double row of kite-shaped plates rising vertically along the rounded back and the two pairs of long spikes extending horizontally near the end of the tail. 1874 Stegosaurus
Triceratops "three-horned face" Bearing a large bony frill, three horns on the skull, and a large, four-legged body, exhibiting convergent evolution with bovines and rhinoceroses, Triceratops is one of the most recognizable of all dinosaurs and the most well-known ceratopsian. 1877 Triceratops
Brachiosaurus "arm lizard" Brachiosaurus was a quadruped with a small skull, a long neck, a large trunk with a high-ellipsoid cross section, a long, muscular tail and slender, columnar limbs. 1903 Brachiosaurus
